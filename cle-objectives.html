Objectives and Beneficiaries



   Objectives
                        

    Study shows that at least 12-18 year olds are diagnosed with depression, although, it can happen to anyone. Our objective is to prevent teens from committing suicide or be aware of fellow peers who suffer from such.

Here is a list of our objectives:

    · The first objective is that we should include suicide prevention as a core component of health care services. Our mental health is just as important as our physical health. Alongside other health care services, they should include free therapy for teens or young children who are suspected to be depressed.

    · The second objective is implement research-informed communication efforts designed to prevent suicide by changing knowledge, attitudes, and behaviors. A lot of teen suffer from depression because of various things such as anxiety, peer pressure, sexuality concerns, a bad home life among many others. Someone who suffers from depression is more unlikely to talk about it. If a teen is suspected to be depressed and refuses to talk about it, the school guidance office or a mental health professional to encourage the teen to confide in them.

    · The last objective is increase knowledge of the factors that offer protection from suicidal behaviors and that promote wellness and recovery. Someone who suffers from depression have a tendency to think about killing themselves to rid of the suffering they’re enduring. Some teens keep to themselves these thoughts while some play it off as “dark humor”. If the school or a concerned parent or anybody at all sees such behavior in a teen, they should be given a gentle talk and convince them to not go through with their plan. They should keep an eye on said teen and prevent them from finding ways to commit suicide.

 		                        
   Specific Beneficiaries
                        

    1. Young children and teens aged 12-18. Study shows that young teens at this age usually get diagnosed with depression. This usually due to pressure in school, bullying, an abusive home and plenty of factors that affect their mental well-being.

    2. Young adults who are diagnosed with depression. This can happen due to personal struggles in life, break-ups or a sudden change in their life like losing a loved one or a close friend.

    3. People who have self-harming issues. This is self-explanatory. People who have issues with self-harm may end up fully committing into harming themselves and in the process, killing themselves.
